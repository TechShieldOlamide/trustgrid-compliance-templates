## 4. Data Deletion Procedures

### 4.1 Secure Deletion Standards

When retention periods expire, data must be deleted using appropriate methods:

| Data Type | Deletion Method |
|-----------|----------------|
| Digital files | Secure overwrite (minimum 3-pass) or cryptographic erasure |
| Database records | Permanent deletion with audit log |
| Physical documents | Cross-cut shredding |
| Storage media | Physical destruction or certified wiping |
| Cloud data | Verified deletion per cloud provider process |

### 4.2 Deletion Process

1. Compliance team identifies data approaching retention expiry
2. Data owner confirms deletion is appropriate
3. Legal hold check — confirm no ongoing litigation or regulatory investigation
4. Deletion executed and documented
5. Certificate of deletion retained for audit purposes

---

## 5. Legal Holds

Data must NOT be deleted if:
- Subject to ongoing litigation or legal proceedings
- Under regulatory investigation or inquiry
- Subject to a court order or preservation notice
- Required for an unresolved insurance claim
