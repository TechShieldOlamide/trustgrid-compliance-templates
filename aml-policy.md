## 4. Transaction Monitoring

### 4.1 Automated Monitoring

Our transaction monitoring system screens for:

| Red Flag | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Structuring | Multiple transactions just below reporting thresholds |
| Unusual volume | Transaction volumes inconsistent with customer profile |
| High-risk geography | Transactions involving sanctioned or high-risk countries |
| Rapid movement | Funds moved in and out quickly with no business purpose |
| Cash intensity | Unusually high cash transaction volumes |

### 4.2 Reporting Thresholds

- Transactions above **NGN 5,000,000** (single or cumulative daily) must be reported to NFIU as a Currency Transaction Report (CTR)
- Suspicious transactions of any amount must be reported as a Suspicious Transaction Report (STR)

---

## 5. Suspicious Activity Reporting

### 5.1 Internal Reporting

Any employee who suspects money laundering must:

1. Complete an internal Suspicious Activity Report (SAR) form
2. Submit to the Money Laundering Reporting Officer (MLRO) within 24 hours
3. Not tip off the customer under any circumstances
4. Preserve all relevant documentation

### 5.2 External Reporting

The MLRO will:
- Review the internal SAR within 5 business days
- File an STR with the NFIU where warranted
- Maintain records of all reports filed and decisions made

## 7. Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)

PEPs and their close associates require:
- Senior management approval for onboarding
- Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)
- Source of funds and wealth documentation
- Continuous enhanced monitoring
- Annual review

---

## 8. Record Keeping

All AML records must be retained for a minimum of **7 years**, including:
- Customer identification documents
- Transaction records
- Internal SARs and decisions
- STRs filed with NFIU
- Training records

---

## 9. Staff Training

All relevant staff must complete AML training:
- At induction
- Annually thereafter
- Following significant regulatory changes

Training records must be maintained and available for regulatory inspection.
