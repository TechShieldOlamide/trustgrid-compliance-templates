## 1. Purpose

This Know Your Customer (KYC) Policy establishes the framework for [COMPANY NAME] to verify the identity of customers, assess risk, and comply with applicable regulations including the Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN) Anti-Money Laundering guidelines, FATF recommendations, and applicable data protection laws.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All new customers onboarding onto [COMPANY NAME] platform
- Existing customers undergoing periodic review
- All staff involved in customer onboarding and verification
- Third-party identity verification partners

## 3. Customer Due Diligence (CDD)

### 3.1 Standard CDD — Required for All Customers

All customers must provide:

| Document Type | Acceptable Forms |
|--------------|-----------------|
| Government-issued photo ID | National ID, International Passport, Driver's Licence, Voter's Card |
| Proof of address | Utility bill, bank statement (not older than 3 months) |
| Date of birth | From ID document |
| BVN (Nigeria) | Bank Verification Number |
| Phone number | Active Nigerian mobile number |
| Email address | Valid and accessible email |

### 3.2 Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) — High Risk Customers

Enhanced verification is required for:

- Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs) and their associates
- Customers from high-risk jurisdictions (FATF grey/black list)
- Transactions above [THRESHOLD AMOUNT]
- Customers flagged by automated risk scoring
- Non-face-to-face onboarding above certain transaction limits

Additional EDD requirements:
- Source of funds declaration
- Source of wealth documentation
- Senior management approval for onboarding
- Enhanced ongoing monitoring

### 3.3 Simplified Due Diligence (SDD)

Simplified verification may apply where:
- Transaction limits are below [LOW THRESHOLD]
- Customer is a regulated financial institution
- Risk assessment confirms low risk profile

---

## 4. Risk-Based Approach

Customers are classified into three risk tiers:

| Risk Tier | Description | Monitoring Level |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| Low | Salaried employees, small transactions, known profiles | Standard |
| Medium | Self-employed, moderate transaction volumes | Enhanced periodic review |
| High | PEPs, cash-heavy businesses, high transaction volumes | Continuous monitoring |

Risk scores are reassessed:
- At onboarding
- Annually for medium and high risk customers
- Upon significant change in customer behaviour or profile

---

## 5. Ongoing Monitoring

[COMPANY NAME] will continuously monitor customer activity to:

- Detect transactions inconsistent with the customer's profile
- Identify unusual patterns or volumes
- Flag suspicious activity for AML review
- Update customer information when changes are identified

Monitoring triggers:
- Transaction amounts exceeding set thresholds
- Multiple transactions just below reporting thresholds (structuring)
- Geographic anomalies
- Changes in transaction patterns

---

## 6. Record Keeping

All KYC documentation must be retained for a minimum of **5 years** after the end of the customer relationship, in accordance with CBN and NDPR requirements.

Records must include:
- Identity documents collected
- Verification results and dates
- Risk assessment outcomes
- Any suspicious activity reports filed
